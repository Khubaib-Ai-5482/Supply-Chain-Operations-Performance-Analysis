# 📦 Supply Chain Operations & Performance Analysis

## Overview

Supply chain operations generate large volumes of data every day, but without proper analysis, organizations struggle to identify inventory issues, evaluate supplier performance, monitor warehouse efficiency, and measure business profitability.

This project uses **SQL and Power BI** to analyze supply chain data and transform raw information into actionable business insights that support operational and strategic decision-making.

---

# Business Problem 1: Inventory Shortage & Overstock Analysis

### Problem

The company has limited visibility into inventory levels across warehouses, making it difficult to identify products that are running out of stock or occupying excessive warehouse space.

### Solution

Inventory levels were analyzed against reorder points to identify low-stock products, overstocked inventory, warehouse-level inventory distribution, and regional inventory shortages.

### Output

* Identified products below the reorder point.
* Detected overstocked products across warehouses.
* Compared inventory shortages by warehouse and region.
* Highlighted products requiring inventory optimization.

---

# Business Problem 2: Supplier Performance Analysis

### Problem

The procurement team lacks visibility into supplier performance, making it difficult to evaluate delivery efficiency and supplier reliability.

### Solution

Supplier lead time, order quantity, and product coverage were analyzed to evaluate supplier performance and identify the most efficient suppliers.

### Output

* Ranked suppliers based on average lead time.
* Identified the fastest and slowest suppliers.
* Measured supplier contribution through order quantity.
* Compared product diversity supplied by each supplier.

---

# Business Problem 3: Warehouse Performance Analysis

### Problem

Management needs better visibility into warehouse operations to improve inventory allocation and operational efficiency.

### Solution

Warehouse performance was evaluated using inventory levels, units sold, order quantity, and regional sales performance.

### Output

* Compared warehouse sales performance.
* Evaluated inventory distribution across warehouses.
* Measured warehouse order processing activity.
* Analyzed regional sales performance.

---

# Business Problem 4: Demand Forecasting Analysis

### Problem

The business needs to compare forecasted demand with actual sales to improve inventory planning and purchasing decisions.

### Solution

Demand forecasts were analyzed alongside actual sales to evaluate forecasting accuracy, promotional impact, and regional demand trends.

### Output

* Compared forecast demand with actual sales.
* Identified high-demand products.
* Measured the impact of promotions on sales.
* Evaluated demand across different regions.

---

# Business Problem 5: Revenue & Profitability Analysis

### Problem

The company requires better financial visibility to identify its highest-performing products, warehouses, and regions.

### Solution

Revenue and profit metrics were analyzed to measure financial performance across different business dimensions.

### Output

* Calculated revenue by warehouse and region.
* Identified the most profitable products.
* Compared warehouse profitability.
* Evaluated overall business performance.
